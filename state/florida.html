Horse Property Insurance in Florida

Florida is home to one of the most concentrated and valuable horse property markets in the world. Marion County and the Ocala area constitute one of the world's premier thoroughbred breeding regions, while Wellington and Palm Beach County host the Winter Equestrian Festival and some of the highest-value show horse facilities in North America. Insuring Florida horse properties requires navigating hurricane exposure, flood risk, and a complex insurance market that has seen significant carrier withdrawals in recent years.

Hurricane Risk

Every Florida horse property faces hurricane exposure. The 2004 and 2005 hurricane seasons caused enormous losses to Florida equestrian facilities, and subsequent seasons have continued to test the market. Key considerations:

  • Hurricane deductibles in Florida are typically expressed as a percentage (2-5%) of insured value rather than a flat dollar amount — on a $500,000 barn, a 2% deductible means $10,000 out of pocket before insurance pays
  • Named storm deductibles may be higher than standard hurricane deductibles
  • Wind mitigation credits are available for structures with qualifying construction features
  • Evacuation planning for horses is essential — carriers may require documented plans for large operations

Flood Risk

Florida's flat terrain and high water table create flood exposure across much of the state. Standard farm and ranch policies exclude flood. Horse properties in FEMA flood zones require separate NFIP or private flood coverage. Many Florida horse properties outside designated flood zones still experience flooding during major rainfall events.

Key Coverage Considerations for Florida Horse Properties

  • Hurricane deductible structure — understand your actual exposure
  • Separate flood coverage — virtually mandatory for Florida horse properties
  • Carrier financial stability — Florida's insurance market has seen insolvencies
  • High-value facility appraisals — Wellington-area facilities can have enormous replacement values
  • Liability for high-traffic competition facilities
