Horse Property Insurance in California

California's horse property insurance market has been fundamentally reshaped by wildfire risk. Following devastating fires in 2017, 2018, 2020, and subsequent years, multiple major carriers have reduced their presence in California's personal lines and farm markets. Horse property owners in high-risk zones — and much of California's horse country falls in those zones — face limited carrier options, higher premiums, and in some cases must rely on the California FAIR Plan as a last resort.

Wildfire Risk Dominates the Market

The California Department of Insurance fire risk maps designate large portions of the state's horse country — including areas around Los Angeles, San Diego, Santa Barbara, the Sierra Nevada foothills, and the North Bay — as High or Very High fire risk zones. Properties in these areas should:

  • Expect premium increases of 30-100%+ compared to low-risk areas
  • Obtain quotes from multiple carriers — availability varies significantly
  • Document all defensible space measures, ember-resistant vents, and fire-resistant materials
  • Maintain current appraisals for all structures — rebuilding costs have risen sharply
California Tip: The California FAIR Plan provides basic fire coverage as a last resort but offers limited coverage types and amounts. Horse property owners relying on FAIR Plan should explore supplemental coverage through the Difference in Conditions (DIC) market for broader protection.

Key Coverage Considerations for California Horse Properties

  • Current replacement cost appraisals — construction costs have escalated significantly
  • Carrier availability in your specific zip code — varies dramatically
  • FAIR Plan alternatives and supplements if standard market unavailable
  • Liability coverage for boarding, training, and lesson operations
  • Water supply and irrigation system coverage
